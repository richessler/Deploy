What is Functional Medicine?

Also called “root cause medicine,” our goal is to identify and address the unique factors contributing to your health, including biochemical individuality, toxic exposures, and genetics. Within the larger field of genetics we focus on the study of epigenetics, which looks at how we can influence the way in which our genes are expressed, and therefore our physiology and disease development. Environmental factors have the ability to send signals to our genes, and those signals will determine if our genetic material is expressed in a way that helps or hinders our health. The way we live, what we eat, the toxins we are exposed to, all break down to chemically provide the microenvironment that bathes our genes and affects disease or health manifestation. The empowering aspect of this kind of focus, is that we are putting the control back into the patient’s own hands, like no other specialty that exists.

Tools for Practice

Through detailed interviewing of an individual’s history and lifestyle we look to uncover factors that may be contributing to your health. In addition to standard tests, we utilize evidence-based lab testing not done by conventional practitioners to open the doors for a wider array of personalized treatment and recommendations. These can include detailed stool analysis, food allergy and sensitivity, heavy metal toxicity, functional nutritional assessments, adrenal evaluation, inflammatory and autoimmune panels, advanced cholesterol subparticle profiles, sex hormone metabolism and expanded thyroid profiles.

Our treatment focus is on nutrition, lifestyle, stress management, herbals and neutraceuticals/supplements, as well as pharmaceutical options when necessary. It is a truly holistic approach, open to incorporating eastern and western medicine options and being directed by the patient’s preference, combined with considerations of safety and efficacy for the health concerns being addressed. The patient-practitioner relationship is paramount and by far the most valuable tool available to promote health and healing. Time is spent to cultivate that relationship and build mutual trust and respect.
