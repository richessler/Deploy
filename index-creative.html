Who I am

I am a board-certified Family Medicine physician, completing my entire education, pre-k through medical residency, in my home state of New Jersey.Being raised by a chiropractor father and homemaker mother who cooked three meals a day from scratch, I was exposed to integrative healthcare practices and learned about the importance of nutrition and “food as medicine” at a young age.

As I started my private practice, I realized my conventional medical training left me feeling void of the ability to truly help those with chronic illness and provide substantial preventative health strategies. In 2010 I started my journey with The Institute for Functional Medicine, an AMA accredited organization, offering evidence-based training for physicians in this field termed “functional medicine.” I knew very quickly that I had found my path to answer the questions my traditional training had failed to address.

Today I am extremely proud to be a certified functional practitioner, with what I believe are the tools to help patients uncover the root causes of their medical issues, as well as try to alleviate dependence on not only pharmaceuticals, but on myself, and all medical providers. I strongly believe in the patient-practitioner partnership and equal responsibility of both parties in the pursuit of health.

What is Functional Medicine?
Also called “root cause medicine,” our goal is to identify and address the unique factors contributing to your health, including biochemical individuality, toxic exposures, and genetics. Within the larger field of genetics we focus on the study of epigenetics, which looks at how we can influence the way in which our genes are expressed, and therefore our physiology and disease development. Environmental factors have the ability to send signals to our genes, and those signals will determine if our genetic material is expressed in a way that helps or hinders our health. The way we live, what we eat, the toxins we are exposed to, all break down to chemically provide the microenvironment that bathes our genes and affects disease or health manifestation. Practice
Through detailed interviewing of an individual’s history and lifestyle we look to uncover factors that may be contributing to your health. In addition to standard tests, we utilize evidence-based lab testing not done by conventional practitioners to open the doors for a wider array of personalized treatment and recommendations. These can include detailed stool analysis, food allergy and sensitivity, heavy metal toxicity, functional nutritional assessments, adrenal evaluation, inflammatory and autoimmune panels, advanced cholesterol subparticle profiles, sex hormone metabolism and expanded thyroid profiles.
Goals
Our treatment focus is on nutrition, lifestyle, stress management, herbals and neutraceuticals/supplements, as well as pharmaceutical options when necessary. It is a truly holistic approach, open to incorporating eastern and western medicine options and being directed by the patient’s preference, combined with considerations of safety and efficacy for the health concerns being addressed. The patient-practitioner relationship is paramount and by far the most valuable tool available to promote health and healing. Time is spent to cultivate that relationship and build mutual trust and respect.
